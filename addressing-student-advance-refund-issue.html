Addressing Student Advance Refund Issue in Rental Property

January 21, 2025

A few days back, 2 students came asking about our rental unit. They are referred by our current tenants (Acharya college students).

They checked the property, and they are fine with the rent and property, but they requested a little discount on the Advance amount.

After a little conversation with the students, I learned their previous owners never returned the Advance they paid, so they have to give less advance as much as possible.

This is a common issue I frequently hear from the students who come asking about our rental units.

This does not only happen in rental units; I also heard similar stories in Paying Guests (PG).

When the students told me the owner hadn’t returned their advance, I told them they might have damaged something in the property, so they could not have returned your security advance.

To which students replied, “No sir, no damages happened” as they replied.

I cannot really comment on hearing one side story. But I know this is commonly happening. I heard many such stories from my current tenants as well.

Let me share my thoughts on it&mldr;

We rent our property from past 4 years. In all these years, I have had almost zero complaints regarding advance amount deductions or advance amount refunds.

Think about ZERO complaints&mldr;

Even if we deduct any money from the advance, we ensure users understand why we are doing it. Before deducting, we give the option to our tenant to fix/replace the damaged part themself. Still, all of the tenants are fine with deducting itself.

In fact, we try to maintain a good relationship with all the tenants even after they vacate our property. They, in fact, refer their friends and ask for any 1 bhk house availability even after moving. If they are angery or upset do you think they will do this?

Don’t get me wrong. We also deduct damages that happened on the property. In most cases, it’s much less. And we refund all the leftover amount.
